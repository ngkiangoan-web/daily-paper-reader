## 摘要
个人光照暴露是影响昼夜节律和神经行为健康的可调节因素，然而其在人群中的日常结构仍知之甚少。我们报告了一项协调一致的多国实地研究，涉及来自哥斯达黎加、德国、加纳、荷兰、西班牙、瑞典和土耳其九个地点的191名成年人，共涵盖1,480个参与日。参与者在角膜平面附近和胸部水平佩戴校准过的光照记录器，并完成重复的情境评估。平均日间光照暴露低于推荐的黑视素光照水平，依从性因地点、每日阶段和光周期而异。尽管低纬度地区全年日照更充足，但光照暴露通常随纬度增加而增加，这表明通过寻找阴影、室内生活或避免高温等行为过滤。个体和活动水平的差异比地点解释了更多变异。日光获取、户外活动和微环境是最强的光照暴露相关因素，而人口因素表现出有限关联。这一公开可访问的数据集提供了首个真实世界眼内光照暴露的多地点视图。

## Abstract
Personal light exposure is a modifiable determinant of circadian and neurobehavioural health, yet its everyday structure across populations remains poorly understood. We report a harmonised, multi-country field study of ocular light exposure in 191 adults across nine sites in Costa Rica, Germany, Ghana, the Netherlands, Spain, Sweden and Turkey, comprising 1,480 participant-days.

Participants wore calibrated light loggers near the corneal plane and at chest level and completed repeated contextual assessments. Average daytime exposure remained below recommended melanopic levels, with adherence varying by site, daily phase and photoperiod. Exposure generally increased with latitude despite greater year-round sunlight at lower latitudes, suggesting behavioural filtering through shade-seeking, indoor living or heat avoidance. Individual and activity-level differences explained more variation than site. Daylight access, outdoor activity and micro-environment were the strongest exposure correlates, whereas demographic factors showed limited associations. This openly accessible dataset provides the first multi-site view of real-world ocular light exposure.
